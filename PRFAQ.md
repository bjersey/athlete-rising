### Problem

Gym owners today face a frustrating choice: use complex enterprise software packed with features they don't need, or cobble together spreadsheets and disconnected tools. Existing solutions are frequently cited for unreliability (downtime during peak hours), confusing interfaces that require extensive training, and pricing that doesn't match the value delivered. New gym owners, meanwhile, struggle to find an affordable system that's easy to set up without a dedicated IT team. Migration from one platform to another is painful, often requiring manual data re-entry and weeks of disruption.

Beyond operations, gym owners lose revenue every day because they lack simple tools to re-engage lapsed members, follow up with interested prospects, or run promotions. Most owners know that a member who hasn't shown up in three weeks is at risk of canceling — but they have no automated way to catch it and act on it. Leads who fill out a website form and don't hear back within days go cold. And running a simple referral discount or limited-time sale often requires a patchwork of email tools, spreadsheets, and manual tracking.

This problem is especially acute in the BJJ space, where the number of academies worldwide has grown to approximately 14,000 and the practitioner base in the US alone surged 75% between 2019 and 2023. Many BJJ academy owners are martial artists first and business operators second — they need software that stays out of the way.

Athlete Rising is a cloud-hosted gym management platform built on AWS that focuses on what gym owners actually need. It provides:

- Membership account management (create, update, pause, cancel)
- Stripe-integrated payment processing for dues, classes, and one-time charges
- Easy migration tools to import data from existing systems with minimal disruption
- Marketing integrations connecting CRMs and website sign-up forms to the membership system
- Member usage tracking — visit frequency, class attendance, and trend analysis
- Member profiles with a complete view of each member's history and engagement
- Business insights dashboard surfacing patterns in attendance, retention, and revenue

  - **Lead nurturing** — Tracks all interest sign-ups from website forms and CRM integrations, and sends periodic follow-up messages to prospects who haven't converted to members
  - **Campaign tools** — Simple setup for limited-time membership sales, referral discount programs, and seasonal promotions — no external email platform required

The platform is designed around simplicity. There are no unnecessary features cluttering the interface. Gym owners can be up and running within a day.

**Q: What are the key risks?**
A:
- **Adoption risk** — Gym owners may be reluctant to switch despite dissatisfaction due to perceived migration effort. Mitigation: guided migration tooling and white-glove onboarding support.
- **Feature gap risk** — Some owners may need features Athlete Rising intentionally excludes (e.g., full POS, retail inventory). Mitigation: focus on core gym management and integrate with third-party tools where needed rather than building everything.
- **Stripe dependency** — Payment processing relies entirely on Stripe. Mitigation: Stripe is highly reliable, but the architecture should allow for adding alternative processors in the future.
- **Messaging deliverability** — Email and SMS outreach effectiveness depends on deliverability and compliance (CAN-SPAM, TCPA). Mitigation: use established messaging infrastructure (e.g., Amazon SES, Twilio/SNS) and build opt-in/opt-out compliance into the platform from day one.
